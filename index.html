About the Gyllencreutz Family

The Gyllencreutz family lineage begins in 16th-century Holstein with Lars Tykesson, a stablemaster to the Duke. His son, Tyke Larson, rose to become a court master for Duke Magnus of Östergötland. The family's history took a pivotal turn on January 26, 1594, when Tyke Larson was granted nobility by King Sigismund at Stockholm Castle. It was not until 1625, upon being introduced to the Swedish House of Nobility, that the family was given the name Gyllencreutz and the number 54. This noble status was formally confirmed by Queen Kristina in 1644, and in 1778, the family was elevated to the prestigious knight class. As a tangible link to this history, the original letter of arms has been carefully preserved at Riddarhuset since 1887.
